What is Cycle Detection?

Cycle detection is the process of identifying recurring patterns in stock price movements. While traditional technical analysis often relies on visual pattern recognition, our approach uses mathematical algorithms to objectively identify cyclical behavior that might be invisible to the naked eye.

Using Fast Fourier Transform (FFT) and other signal processing techniques, we can detect:

  • Dominant Cycles - The most significant recurring patterns in the price data
  • Cycle Lengths - The time period of each cycle (e.g., 20-day,.40-day cycles)
  • Cycle Strength - How pronounced each cycle is within the overall price movement
  • Current Position - Where in the cycle the current price is positioned

How Our Cycle Detection Works

  1. Data Preprocessing

    First, we clean and normalize the price data to remove irregularities and prepare it for analysis. We apply detrending to separate cyclical patterns from long-term trends.

  2. Fast Fourier Transform (FFT)

    We apply FFT to transform the price data from the time domain to the frequency domain. This mathematical operation reveals which cycle lengths (frequencies) are most prominent in the data.

  3. Peak Detection

    Our algorithm identifies peaks in the frequency spectrum that represent dominant cycles. Each peak corresponds to a cycle of a specific length.

  4. Cycle Ranking

    We rank cycles by their strength (amplitude) and filter out noise to focus on the most significant patterns.

  5. Phase Analysis

    For each identified cycle, we calculate its current phase, which tells us where in the cycle the price currently is (e.g., bottom, rising, top, or falling).

Why Cycle Detection Matters

Market cycles occur due to various factors including institutional behavior, market psychology, and seasonal patterns. By detecting these cycles objectively, traders can make more informed decisions about market timing and anticipate potential turning points.

Applications in Trading

Cycle detection can be applied to various trading strategies:

Counter-Trend Trading

Identify potential reversal points by looking for prices approaching cycle tops or bottoms.

Trend Confirmation

Use cycle analysis to confirm trend direction and strength, especially when multiple cycles align.

Cycle Period Estimation

Estimate how long current market conditions might persist based on dominant cycle lengths.

Risk Management

Adjust position sizes and stop losses based on cycle volatility and expected turning points.
