Cycle-Based Trading Signals

Our trading signals are generated through a comprehensive analysis of detected market cycles and their current positions. By understanding where price is located within multiple cycles of different lengths, we can provide actionable recommendations with confidence ratings.

Types of Trading Signals

Buy Signal

Generated when multiple cycles are at or near their bottom phases, suggesting potential upward movement.

Ideal for: Entering new long positions or adding to existing ones.

Hold Signal

Generated when cycles are in transitional phases or when different cycles are giving conflicting signals.

Ideal for: Maintaining current positions without increasing exposure.

Sell Signal

Generated when multiple cycles are at or near their peak phases, suggesting potential downward movement.

Ideal for: Exiting long positions or considering short positions.

Confidence Ratings

Each trading signal comes with a confidence percentage that indicates the strength and clarity of the signal. This helps you gauge the reliability of the recommendation.

High Confidence (80-100%)
  • Multiple strong cycles aligned in the same direction
  • Clear cycle patterns with minimal noise
  • Historical reliability of detected cycles
  • Strong signal intensity
Medium Confidence (60-79%)
  • Some cycle alignment with mixed signals
  • Moderate cycle clarity
  • Some historical consistency
  • Average signal intensity
Low Confidence (Below 60%)
  • Conflicting cycle signals
  • Weak or noisy cycle patterns
  • Limited historical consistency
  • Low signal intensity
